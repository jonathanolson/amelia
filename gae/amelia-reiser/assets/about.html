Amelia Reiser

Amelia Reiser is a native of Norman, OK, where she began dancing in the front yard with her sister as early as she could walk. Her formal dance training began under the guidance of Julie Blacksten at the Classical Ballet Academy in 1991.

Amelia continued to explore and investigate her passion for dance and has received a BFA in Dance from the University of Georgia. While at UGA, Amelia was involved in the CORE Concert Dance Company under the artistic direction of Bala Sarasvati, the UGA Ballet Ensemble with Joan Buttram, and performed and choreographed for the Young Choreographer’s Series: Emerging Choreographers and Senior Exit Concerts.

Amelia attended the University of Oklahoma in the spring of 2009 where she studied Horton technique under Derrick Minter. While in Oklahoma, she also trained and performed in trapeze dance with Perpetual Motion Modern Dance out of Oklahoma City.

In 2010-2011, Amelia worked with Diavolo Dance Theater and performed AWOL and collaborated to create and perform Pandora’s Box as a part of Company B in Athens, GA. Amelia has expanded upon her dance training at various festivals including Bates Dance Festival and The American Dance Festival.

Recently Amelia performed works by UGA faculty Rebecca Enghauser, Janet Robertson, and Darly Foster in the Spring Dance Concert 2011. She has also collaborated with Heather McIntosh to organize and perform an improvised dance study and have her screen dance Hit. shown at the AUX Experimental Arts Festival in Athens, GA.
