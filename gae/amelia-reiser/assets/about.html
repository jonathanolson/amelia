Amelia Reiser is an Atlanta based dancer, choreographer, and filmmaker. A native of Norman, Oklahoma, Amelia began dancing in the kitchen with her older sister just as soon as she could stand up. Her formal dance training began under the guidance of Julie Blacksten at the Classical Ballet Academy in 1991.

Amelia continued to explore her passion for movement and has received a BFA in Dance from the University of Georgia. While at UGA, Amelia trained in aerial and contemporary dance with the CORE Concert Dance Company under the artistic direction of Bala Sarasvati. She also trained with the UGA Ballet Ensemble under the direction of Joan Buttram, performed and choreographed for the Young Choreographer’s Series and Senior Exit Concerts, and performed works by Rebecca Enghauser, Janet Robertson, and Daryl Foster in the Dance Reparatory Project Spring Concert 2011.

In 2010-2011, Amelia worked with Diavolo Dance Theater and performed AWOL, and collaborated to create and perform Pandora’s Box as a part of Company B in Athens, GA. Amelia has expanded upon her dance training at various festivals including Bates Dance Festival and The American Dance Festival.

In addition to creating and performing in live contemporary dance, Amelia is currently exploring the impact of dance on film; the environment editing can create with movement, and making site-specific work accessible to large audiences. Amelia has created numerous short dance films, both alone and collaboratively with other artists.

Amelia’s work has been shown at various festivals including I.F. O.N.L.Y International Festival of a Necessarily Lonely You (Birr, Ireland) and AUX Experimental Arts Festival (Athens, GA). Her most recent collaboration with visual artist Lesley Wingfiled, a short underwater dance film titled Waters and the Wild (2012 Athlone, Ireland) has been shown at the Lightwell Gallery (Norman, OK) and at the Luan Gallery (Athlone, Ireland)
